Pirate Aggro

The premiere aggro burn list that remains resilient even after 3 years since this deck's inception. The name of the game is to count down from 20 life. You play aggressive one drops, make wide swing open attacks, and consistently draw and tutor out your top end burn options to close out games.

Red Gwen

This was the game's boogieman during the last stretch of 2022's LoR world championship and for good reason. The deck's match-up spread was practically unmatched. With the flexibility to pivot into any deck speed to accommodate certain hand states or even specific match-ups. The region combination of Noxus/Shadow Isles also held access to the game's best aggro/control region card pools respectively. Many high level players who committed to this deck created many versions that held a lot of room for flexible tech cards to catch the meta off guard.

AzIrelia

AzIrelia. Oh AzIrelia... I know I said that Red Gwen was the 2022 boogie man but this deck is the first deck that most LoR players will immediately exclaim when asked which deck is never allowed to return. This deck, on release, at full power, was unmistakably the strongest deck in LoR and was allowed to keep its tier 1 status, for over 6 months. With 10 individual nerfs during those 6 months. It took a whole new set to push the deck out of the meta, and even with its power-level drastically cut, it remains as a strong all in combo deck that can still perform.
